General


The grey mullet (Mugil cephalus) is widespread throughout the world. In New Zealand it is most common in northern harbours, and is often caught with set nets.


Facts


Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Actinopterygii

Order: Mugiliformes
Family: Mugilidae

Genus: Mugil

Species: Mugil Cephalus


The Grey Mullet is a mainly diurnal coastal species that often enters estuaries and rivers. It usually schools over sand or mud bottoms, feeding on zooplankton. The Adult fish normally feed on algae in fresh water. The maximum it may reach is approximately 120 cm, with a max weight of about 8,000 g. The species is euryhaline meaning that the fish can acclimate to different levels of salinity; this combined with the acclimation of juveniles to high water temperatures appears to be a selective advantage


Description


The Grey Mullet have dark centers which give the appearance of a series (6-7) of dark horizontal stripes. The fish grow to lengths up to 60.0 cm (24 inches) with weights as high as 4.0 kg (8.5 pounds).


Habitat


The Grey Mullet normally occupies fresh, brackish and marine habitats in depths ranging between 0–120 m and with temperatures between 8-24°C.
